Introduction
 
In 2002, we commemorate numerous historical milestones in the development of the Rieter CZ company. Above all, it is the period of ten years since signing of the co-operation contract between the Elitex state company and the Rieter concern. In 1992, this resolved the way of privatisation and the present phase of the growth of all four plants. Fifty years ago, in 1952, the state decided to lay the foundations of textile engineering in the region of Ústí nad Orlicí. However, without the long tradition of textile industry based on the local workers and entrepreneurs, textile engineering could not reach such achievements which we still continue to develop today. Therefore, it is natural to acknowledge these traditions and to remember dates even 120 years old. The most important of all is the view of the future which should not find us unprepared but able to react to changes in time, or even better, to participate actively on the progress.
 
Václav Klička                                                     Ústí nad Orlicí 7. března 2002

Conclusion
 
Dear readers,
if this book has completed your knowledge of less familiar details concerning traditions of textile engineering and has also inspired you to think about how ready we are for the future in times of ever more dynamic advancement of new technologies at the beginning of the third millennium, then this book has satisfied its purpose.
The joint aim of authors was to summarise facts and relations regarding the development of textile engineering in areas of Ústí nad Orlicí, Žamberk, and Choceň, where today’s factories of the Rieter CZ company operate. I am very glad that experienced historians undertook to document events of social and economic development to form a frame for the description of the present shape of the Rieter CZ company. The second part of the book includes an overview of ideas and technical solutions whose potential has been utilised for the progression of the whole textile engineering field and which have been realised right in the company since the start of the Kovostav plant in 1952. This part was written by a well-known specialist in rotor spinning who directly participated in the development.
The process of privatisation of the Elitex state company, another significant milestone, was initiated in 1992. Therefore this book is dedicated to this ten-year anniversary. In conclusion, I thank co-authors and everyone who has supported the birth of this book or has contributed to enrich its contents in any way.
Please let me add a note on the technical aspect of this book’s publication. To be consistent with the opening idea of the upcoming digital age, the first edition of this book is being published in the form of an e-book. I hope that this has a premiere not only within the concern, but it will also gain its historical priority in the area of Czech publications in the filed of textile engineering.
Although the e-book should not replace a printed book, there are some pictures in the introduction. Numerous illustrations, charts, graphs, and photographs, including the complete bibliography are prepared for possible publication.
Finally, I would like to apologise for technical imperfections of this edition, whose language has not been checked. However, I do believe that readers will enjoy the content and design of this e-book.
 
Václav Klička
